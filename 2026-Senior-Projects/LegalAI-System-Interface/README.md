# LegalAI: RAG-Driven Legal Translation System
**Graduation Project | KOREATECH 2025-2026**

**Collaboration:** Team Project (Lead Mobile Architect & UI/UX Designer)  
**Tech Stack:** `Flutter`, `Dart`, `Llama 3 (8B)`, `RAG Architecture`, `VS Code`  
**Domain:** Natural Language Processing (NLP), Mobile Software Engineering, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)

---

### Project Overview
LegalAI is a specialized intelligence platform designed to improve legal accessibility for foreign residents in South Korea. By leveraging **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** and domain-specific **Llama 3** fine-tuning, the system provides high-fidelity translations anchored in verified legal databases, addressing the accuracy and "hallucination" limitations found in general-purpose translation tools.

---

## 2025: Engineering Design Phase (공학설계)
*Focus: Comparative Research, Requirement Analysis, and Theoretical Foundation*

The initial phase focused on identifying the failures of current translation technologies in the legal domain and defining a technical architecture to solve them.

* **Comparative App Evaluation**: Conducted a deep-dive analysis of existing translation platforms to identify specific failure points and inaccuracies when handling complex Korean legal terminology.
* **Strategic Vision**: Defined the social impact and expected outcomes of the system, focusing on bridging the legal information gap for the non-native population in Korea.
* **Academic Validation**: Presented the conceptual framework and system requirements via a final poster presentation, which was evaluated and graded by a panel of five professors.
* **Evaluation Framework**: Established the use of quantitative metrics, including **COMET**, **BERTScore**, and **BLEU**, to validate translation accuracy against official government records.

---

## 2026: Implementation Phase (Senior Project/졸업설계)
*Focus: System Architecture, UI/UX Design, and Cross-Platform Development*

This phase involves transforming research concepts into a functional product. The development process required a complete re-architecture of the client-side system to ensure production-grade stability and a human-centered user experience.

### **Lead Mobile Architect & UI/UX Designer**
*Responsibility for the end-to-end frontend architecture and the visual representation of the AI-driven system.*

* **System Re-Architecture**: Re-architected the mobile application from the ground up using **Flutter** and **Dart**. This involved replacing the initial prototype skeleton with a stable application structure designed to handle complex legal data formats and facilitate intuitive user interaction.
* **UI/UX Benchmarking**: Analyzed interface patterns from leading AI applications to implement intuitive user flows for chat-based interactions and legal document navigation.
* **Design Methodology & Visual Identity**:
    * Utilized a multi-tool design workflow to establish a high-trust, readable visual system.
    * **Realtime Colors**: Main tool for visualizing component-level color interactions and typography in real-time.
    * **Figma Color Wheel**: Used for establishing mathematical color harmonies and balanced palettes.
    * **Colorffy & UIcolors.app**: Leveraged for generating accessible, Tailwind-scale color palettes and theme inspiration.
* **Interface Implementation**:
    * Engineered the **Main Dashboard**, **Chat Interface**, and **Sign-In** modules.
    * Developed the logic for the visual architecture of translations, ensuring an organized and clear presentation of RAG-generated content within the UI.
    * Managed the development lifecycle using **VS Code** and **Android Studio emulators** for cross-platform testing.

### **Quantitative Performance Evaluation**
* **Research Validation**: Leading the final phase of the project to measure the system's effectiveness.
* **Metric Analysis**: Utilizing **COMET**, **BERTScore**, and **BLEU** to conduct a formal performance evaluation, comparing the RAG-driven outputs against baseline models and official legal translations.
